Strategy Story.

Imagine a trader named Alex sitting in front of two screens.

One screen shows **Polymarket**.

The other shows **real-time Chainlink prices** for BTC and ETH.

Every 5 minutes, a new game begins.

---

### The Game Starts

At exactly 12:00:00, Polymarket creates a market:

> "Will Bitcoin be above $100,000 at 12:05?"

The starting price ($100,000) becomes the **strike price**.

Alex's bot writes it down and then waits.

It doesn't rush in.

It doesn't care what happens during the first few minutes.

It just watches.

---

### Minute 1, 2, 3...

Bitcoin moves around.

$100,010.

$99,980.

$100,040.

Nothing interesting yet.

The bot keeps watching.

It knows there is still plenty of time for the market to change direction.

A trade now would be gambling.

---

### Final 40 Seconds

Now things get interesting.

The bot looks at Bitcoin.

Strike price: **$100,000**

Current price: **$100,085**

Bitcoin is clearly above the line.

But the bot doesn't trust Bitcoin alone.

It turns to Ethereum.

---

### The Confirmation

Ethereum's strike was:

$3,000

Current price:

$3,008

Ethereum is also above its strike.

The bot smiles.

Both friends are walking in the same direction.

BTC says "UP."

ETH says "UP."

That is stronger than one asset moving alone.

---

### Looking Into The Crowd

Now the bot looks at the Polymarket order book.

It wants to see if traders are behaving normally.

Questions:

* Is there enough liquidity?
* Are spreads tight?
* Are buyers and sellers active?
* Is the YES side healthy?

Everything looks good.

---

### The Double Check

The bot doesn't jump immediately.

It waits for another market tick.

A fraction of a second later:

BTC still above strike.

ETH still above strike.

Books still healthy.

Signal confirmed twice.

Now the bot acts.

---

### The Purchase

With 25 seconds left before expiry, the bot buys:

> YES

at around 0.99.

Many people would ask:

> "Why buy at 0.99? There's almost no profit!"

Because the bot isn't looking for a jackpot.

It's looking for something that is *almost already decided*.

The bot is basically saying:

> "I'm paying 99 cents because I think there's a very good chance this will become worth 100 cents in a few seconds."

Tiny profit.

High probability.

---

### Disaster Check

Immediately after buying, the bot becomes paranoid.

It asks itself:

> "What if I'm wrong?"

Every fraction of a second it checks:

* Did the bid collapse?
* Did liquidity disappear?
* Is something strange happening?

If danger appears, it exits.

No emotions.

No hope.

No praying.

Just sell.

---

### The Clock Runs Down

15 seconds left.

10 seconds left.

5 seconds left.

Bitcoin remains above strike.

Ethereum remains above strike.

No crash.

No surprise reversal.

---

### The Finish Line

The epoch ends.

Bitcoin closes at:

$100,078

Above strike.

YES wins.

The position settles successfully.

The bot records a small profit.

Not a huge one.

Just a tiny gain.

Then it forgets everything and prepares for the next 5-minute game.

---

### The Bot's Philosophy

Most traders try to predict the future.

This bot does something different.

It waits until the future is almost here.

Instead of asking:

> "Where will Bitcoin be in 5 minutes?"

it asks:

> "There are only a few seconds left. Is the answer already obvious?"

If BTC and ETH both agree, and the order books agree, and the market still offers a trade, the bot steps in for a few seconds and tries to collect a tiny edge.

Then it leaves before the next battle begins.
